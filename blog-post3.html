What to Do If Insurance Refuses to Pay

By DriveRight Legal Team • 4 min read

Insurance claim rejection is one of the most common and frustrating experiences for drivers. While insurers have the right to investigate claims, they are legally required to act in good faith. Not every refusal is lawful.

1. Common Reasons Insurance Claims Are Rejected

Insurance companies often cite reasons such as lack of evidence, policy exclusions, late reporting, or disputed fault. While some rejections are valid, others are based on vague or questionable grounds.

Drivers should carefully review the written explanation provided by the insurer and compare it with the policy terms.

2. The Legal Duty of Insurers

Under insurance law, insurers must handle claims fairly and transparently. This includes properly investigating claims and clearly explaining any denial decisions.

Unreasonable delays, ignoring evidence, or misinterpreting policy terms may constitute bad faith conduct.

3. Real Case Example

In a common dispute, a driver’s claim was rejected due to “insufficient proof” of damage. However, the driver had submitted photos, repair estimates, and a police report.

After filing a written appeal and resubmitting the evidence, the insurer reversed its decision and paid the claim in full.

4. Legal Steps Drivers Can Take

Drivers have the right to formally appeal insurance decisions. This often involves submitting a written objection supported by evidence and policy references.

If the dispute remains unresolved, drivers may escalate the matter to regulatory authorities or courts, depending on jurisdiction.

5. When an Insurer Is Acting Unlawfully

Courts often rule against insurers when refusals are based on incorrect policy interpretation, missing investigations, or intentional delay tactics.

Understanding these legal principles empowers drivers to challenge unfair claim denials and protect their rights.
